Introduction: I have created a website for a fashion and lifestyle brand called ‘Waves’. It’s a blog type website where you can know about their operations, facilities and stories. It’s a simple website where I created different pages (accessible from the top navigation menu) for different purposes. This website tells the story about trending fashion, styles, appearances and lifestyle goals. I choose to implement a blog type structure. I think it’s the best way to represent the story I want to tell through my website.

Inspirations: I got inspired from these three websites, which are:

  1. https://mettro.com.au/
  2. https://wovenmagazine.com/
  3. https://www.vogue.co.uk/

Accessibility: My site is accessible in the following ways:

  1. The site is accessible for People using mobile phones, tablets, smart TVs, and other devices with small screens, different input modes, etc.

  2. Alternative text for images: People who cannot see and use a screen reader that reads aloud the information on a page, including the alt text for the visual image.

  3. The Web be accessible in order to provide equal access and equal opportunity to people with diverse abilities.

Usability: I considered the following 3 principles of good website usability:

  1. I tried to ensure my visitors don’t get an error trying to load the site and my site can handle different screen sizes.

  2. I ensured good ‘clarity’ of my website so that visitors can reach their goals and find their desired content as quickly as possible.

  3. I also tried to ensure that my website contents are “relevant” to the user demand.

Learning: I learned some techniques while creating this website. I am mentioning three of them here:

  1. Masonry Library: I learned how to use masonry library to create grid-style contents easily. I used Masonry Library for my homepage.

  2. Swiper Slider: I also learned about the swiper slider and how to implement it to slide images continuously on the website. I used this on several pages of my website.

  3. Sidebar: I learned how to implement sidebar. I decided size for each portion and arrange the contents accordingly. I used this on some of my pages.

Evaluation 1: I learned some techniques while creating this website. I am mentioning three of them here: I think my content arrangement (grid) and spacing was successful. I understood how to use white-space and space between items quite fairly and implemented these successfully on the website.

Evaluation 2: Responsiveness across multiple devices could be improved in my opinion. I am kind of new to this, I tried to do what I could. I believe there’s a lot of room for improvement. In future I will try to make my website more responsive and I will try to use frameworks like Bootstrap, Pure CSS, Foundation etc to make my website better.
